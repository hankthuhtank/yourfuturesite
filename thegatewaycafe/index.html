Why "Gateway"?

The gateway to the Judith Basin.

Long before the diner, Hobson was the freight-wagon crossing of the Judith River — the way through, between Fort Benton and the Musselshell country. Folks called it the Gateway to the Judith Basin. The café took the name and ran with it.

Today it's a vintage-'50s diner: teal and chrome, checkerboard floors, a classic car on the sign, and a record always ready to spin. Family-friendly, open early and late, and known around Judith Basin County for the best burger you'll find for miles — plus boba, Italian sodas, and milkshakes thick enough to stand a spoon in.

A welcome break from the long Montana road, with service that's warm and personable — the rare kind that makes 179 people feel like a crowd.
